Five Iconic African Destinations

Zanzibar, Tanzania
01
🇹🇿

Zanzibar

The Spice Island · Indian Ocean

Nestled 36km off the Tanzanian coast, Zanzibar is a breathtaking archipelago of powder-white beaches and turquoise water. The historic Stone Town — a UNESCO World Heritage Site — weaves together Arab, Persian, Indian and Swahili cultures in a labyrinth of narrow streets and ornately carved doors. Known as the Spice Island, Zanzibar was once the world's leading clove producer. Today it offers world-class snorkeling, diving, and the most memorable sunsets on the African continent.

Stone Town UNESCO Spice Farms Nungwi Beach Snorkeling & Diving Dolphin Tours

Mauritius, Indian Ocean
02
🇲🇺

Mauritius

Island Paradise · South Indian Ocean

A volcanic island floating in the south Indian Ocean, Mauritius is a captivating blend of cultures — African, Indian, French and British influences fuse into something utterly unique. Ringed almost entirely by coral reefs and lagoons of impossibly clear turquoise water, this island offers luxury five-star resorts, world-class golf and spectacular water sports alongside majestic mountain ranges, dense rainforests and sugar-cane plantations. Perfect for families, honeymooners and adventurers alike.

Luxury Resorts Chamarel Waterfall Black River Gorges Water Sports Creole Cuisine

Seychelles · 115 Islands
03
🇸🇨

Seychelles

Exclusive Island Eden · Indian Ocean

An archipelago of 115 extraordinary islands northeast of Madagascar, the Seychelles is the world's most exclusive island paradise. Dramatic granite boulders frame beaches of the softest white sand, lapped by waters of impossible blue. Three main islands — Mahé, Praslin and La Digue — each offer a distinct experience, from vibrant capital Victoria to the UNESCO-listed Vallée de Mai, home of the legendary coco de mer palm. The ultimate destination for honeymooners, luxury seekers and nature lovers.

Anse Lazio Beach Vallée de Mai UNESCO Island Hopping Marine Reserves Giant Tortoises

Kenya, East Africa
04
🇰🇪

Kenya Safari

The Great Migration · Masai Mara

Kenya is the birthplace of the safari dream. The legendary Masai Mara National Reserve hosts the Greatest Show on Earth — the annual Great Migration of over 1.5 million wildebeest and hundreds of thousands of zebra crossing the Mara River in a thundering spectacle unlike anything else on the planet. Beyond the Mara, Amboseli offers iconic views of Kilimanjaro framed by elephant herds, while the Maasai people offer a living cultural encounter of extraordinary richness. The Big Five awaits.

Great Migration Big Five Safari Masai Mara Amboseli NP Maasai Culture

Cape Town, South Africa
05
🇿🇦

Cape Town

The Mother City · South Africa

Consistently voted one of the world's most beautiful cities, Cape Town sits beneath the iconic flat-topped Table Mountain where the Atlantic and Indian Oceans converge. The Mother City dazzles with Boulders Beach penguins, the Cape Winelands of Stellenbosch and Franschhoek, vibrant Victoria & Alfred Waterfront, and the historic Robben Island. Nearby Kruger National Park offers South Africa's premier Big Five safari experience. Cape Town is Africa at its most spectacular and accessible.

Table Mountain Cape Winelands Kruger National Park V&A Waterfront Robben Island
